## 3. SHEEP BREEDS

### Black Welsh Mountain Sheep
- One of only approximately 50 registered flocks in the United States
- Recently secured a new bloodline directly from the UK
- Small dual-purpose breed: excellent mild mutton + completely black, dense, durable fleece
- Small and easy to handle (mature ewe ~100 lbs, mature ram 132-143 lbs)
- Rams have attractive horns that curl around ears; ewes are polled (hornless)
- Hardy, self-reliant, excellent foragers, excellent mothers
- Breed status: "Threatened" (The Livestock Conservancy)
- First imported to North America in 1972 by Thomas Wyman of Easton, Maryland
- US breed registry established in 1990

### Romney Sheep
- Longwool breed from Kent, England
- Dual-purpose: meat and wool
- Known for heavy, thick fleece
- Well-known for spinning and hand-knitting
- Rockbridge Farm supplies Romney fleeces to Ross Farm Mercantile (Washington County, PA) for their yarn products
